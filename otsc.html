Operational Technology Support Centre

We help organisations find their true potential with our customised technologies and end-to-end support models. This gives our customers greater visibility and insight of their internal and external operations.

    Reduce number of personnel required to respond to an incident on the ground

    Reduce opportunity cost of the management of incidents by removing interface barriers

    Optimize network availability by reducing MTTR

    Reduction in maintenance and support staff for OEM's on-site

    Accelerate Root Cause Analysis and generate lead indicators for automation of responses and rectification process

    Retain developed knowledge in the organization

    Enable the drivers for the change to Autonomous Technology to be achieved

    Reduction in support costs by maintaining a single truth source

    FTP Solutions has Operational Technology Support Centers (OTSC) to provide remote Tier 3 support services. With OTSC’s based in both Perth, WA and Calgary, Alberta we can offer a cost-effective 24/7/365 follow-the-sun model which can provide always-on monitoring.

    Our proposed model of internal staff engagement involves rotating our site based operational support team with our OTSC technical staff at our home office locations to ensure a familiarity of site-based processes and operations methodology to bring maximum value to our clients.

    By providing remote operations support to multiple AHS customers globally, FTP Solutions is uniquely positioned to provide expertise that is experientially based. When issues are observed at one site, our expertise can permeate this knowledge to each other customer site to ensure optimum availability and performance for all our clients.

OTSC Accountabilities

Triggers and Alerts

The customer IMS instance comes preconfigured with some FTP Solutions’ recommended triggers and alerts. These are set at generic predetermined thresholds in order to provide the client with some basic monitoring tools. These can be edited, fine-tuned and removed at the discretion of any user, with the idea being, that alerts will be built upon to refine the monitoring of a site’s operations.

Daily health Checks

The OTSC will carry out daily health checks and review the last 24hr reporting period, reviewing low-priority/low-significance events and assessing site network performance, irrespective if IMS generated alarms or a customer e-mailed or called to log a ticket. If any issues are noted, then a ticket may be generated, or a designated customer contact may be notified. This ensures that potential problems are still being addressed if the existing trigger and alert configuration is not fully refined.

Incident Management

If and when an asset or device/s exhibits failures that are impacting, or likely to impact operations, the OTSC can coordinate the correct onsite resources to respond appropriately. This means that there are less unnecessary actions taking place including the mobilization of incorrect support resources, the creation of job tickets; eliminating the “blame game” that is invariably played out when a truck stops for example.

Post Incident Review

Following an event or incident with an onsite asset, whether it was with the supporting network, an application error or any kind of fault with OEM/hardware, a post incident review will take place. From this a summary report detailing the sequence of events that lead to the discovery of the issue will be generated and distributed, including a review of what went wrong, a list of the actions taken to recover, and any temporary work arounds that have been implemented.

Problem management

The OTSC has the visibility across multiple shifts and rosters, easily corroborating frequent and infrequent incidents that may highlight a common problem. Problems that have been identified are reported to the appropriate authority, raising a change or making the necessary adjustments, minimizing the frequency of outages and potential downtime. The impact of this is further amplified in an enterprise with multiple sites with OTSC support.

Performance Reporting

A biweekly review of the customer’s network performance is constructed detailing trends, incidents and observations for each site with the OTSC service. This performance report can be tailored to each site to include any IMS collected data and includes OTSC recommended improvements.

Network Troubleshooting Level 1 Support

Level 1 support will identify issues via the relevant triggers and alarms in IMS and/or by taking calls and emails from the customer. The OTSC tech will collaborate with the customer’s on-site technical resources investigating what is causing identified fault.

Network Troubleshooting Level 2/3 Support

Unresolved tickets may be escalated to a Level 2 or Level 3 Engineer for assessment. (full network access required to complete this function to full capability) Level 2/3 will use all avenues available to resolve the issue remotely. Should resolution at level 2/3 not be possible, OTSC can escalate to the appropriate Level 4 resource.

Network Troubleshooting Level 4 Support

As a part of providing remote services, the OTSC will take full accountability to manage interfaces with the Level 4 OEM interface regardless of the vendor. If the issue is deemed to be a network hardware specific issues, the OTSC will leverage the existing support agreement that the client has with each third-party network hardware manufacturer to escalate and bring to issue resolution. Full accountability of this third-party interface with respect to network hardware, or OEM escalation with the autonomy provider is included within OTSC’s mandate.
